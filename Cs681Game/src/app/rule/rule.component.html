Othello Rules

  • The game is played on an 8x8 board with 64 discs, which are black on one side and white on the other.
  • Players take turns placing discs on the board, with their color facing up. Each player starts with two discs in the center of the board.
  • The player with black disc goes first followed by player with white disc and so on...
  • A move is legal if it results in at least one of the opponent's discs being flipped over to the player's color. This can happen horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • If a player cannot make a legal move, their turn is skipped.
  • The game ends when the board is full or when neither player can make a legal move.
  • The player with the most discs of their color on the board at the end of the game wins.

For more information and strategy tips, visit the Othello/Reversi page on Wikipedia.
